# A Working Mythopoet's Survey of Sevens: A Taxonomy of Heptads Across World Cultures

This catalog complements the deep-dive document on the canonical heptads (Genesis creation, Enuma Elish, the classical planets, Lurianic shmita, the Mi'raj heavens). It aims at *breadth*: every set of seven notable enough to register, briefly noted with source and approximate period of attestation. Entries already covered in your deep-dive are referenced only when a fresh angle is available.

## 1. Cosmogonic & Cosmological Sevens

- **Seven Amesha Spentas / Seven Creations** (Zoroastrianism). Ahura Mazda and six "Bounteous Immortals," each yoked to one of the seven primal creations: sky, water, earth, plant, animal, human, fire. Alluded to in the Gathas (c. 1200 BCE); systematized in the Middle Persian *Bundahishn* (9th–12th c. CE).
- **Sapta Loka / Sapta Patala** (Hinduism). Seven upper worlds (Bhur, Bhuvar, Svar, Mahar, Jana, Tapa, Satya) and seven netherworlds (Atala through Patala). Atharvaveda and the Puranas (c. 1000 BCE–1000 CE).
- **Seven heavens** in Jewish, Christian, Islamic, Mandaean, and Hermetic cosmologies — already covered, but note the **Hekhalot** "Seven Palaces" variant in Merkavah mysticism (3rd–8th c. CE) and the **Mandaean seven Maṭarata** (purgatorial way-stations) in the *Ginza Rabba*.
- **Seven earths** (*sheva aratzot*) of midrashic and Islamic cosmology (Talmud Avodah Zarah 41a; Qur'an 65:12).
- **Seven seas** (saptasagara): in Hindu cosmology, concentric oceans of salt, sugarcane-juice, wine, ghee, curds, milk, and fresh water surrounding seven island-continents (Puranas). The Greco-Roman "Seven Seas" was a Mediterranean/Adriatic register; modern usage names the seven oceans.
- **Seven rivers** (*Sapta Sindhu*) of the Rigveda (c. 1500 BCE); also the seven sacred rivers (Ganga, Yamuna, Godavari, Sarasvati, Narmada, Sindhu, Kaveri).
- **Seven mountains** of Puranic geography (Sumeru-girded Kulaparvatas); the **Seven Hills of Rome** (Palatine, Aventine, Capitoline, Quirinal, Viminal, Esquiline, Caelian — fixed by Varro, 1st c. BCE); the **Seven Hills of Istanbul/Constantinople**.
- **Seven dvīpas** (island-continents) of Jambudvipa cosmology (Vishnu Purana).
- **Seven Gates of the Underworld** in *Inanna's Descent* (Sumerian, c. 1900 BCE), at each of which the goddess shedded a garment.
- **Seven Anunnaki judges** of the underworld in the same myth and in the Babylonian *Descent of Ishtar*.
- **Seven Maskim / Sebettu**: the seven evil spirits (or, in benign aspect, seven warrior gods) of Akkadian incantations, second millennium BCE.
- **Seven Hathors** of Egypt (New Kingdom, c. 1500 BCE onward): goddesses who appear at a child's birth to pronounce its fate, named in the *Tale of the Doomed Prince*.
- **Seven Bau** and **Seven Souls of Ra** in Egyptian funerary texts.
- **Pleiades** (the "Seven Sisters"): identified as seven nearly everywhere humans look — Greek daughters of Atlas (Hesiod, *Works and Days*, c. 700 BCE); Vedic Krittikā (wives of the Saptarishi, nurses of Kartikeya); Japanese **Subaru**; Aboriginal Australian "Seven Sisters" Dreaming songlines; Lakota star-knowledge; Maori Matariki. Common archaeological motif since the **Nebra sky disk** (c. 1600 BCE).
- **Big Dipper / Saptarishi**: the seven stars as the Seven Sages in India; as the "Seven Oxen" (*septem triones*, source of "northern") in Latin; as the imperial bushel in Daoist astronomy; as the soul-carrier of the Lakota Oceti Sakowin.
- **Seven classical planets** (covered) and their day-names that gave modern weekday nomenclature in Latinate, Germanic, Japanese, and Indian languages.

## 2. Abrahamic & Western Religious Sevens

- **Seven days of creation** (Genesis 1, c. 6th c. BCE) — covered.
- **Seven Noahide laws** (Talmud Sanhedrin 56a); seven Canaanite nations driven out (Deut. 7:1); seven branches of the **Menorah** (Exodus 25); the **shmita** sabbatical year and the **Jubilee** after seven sevens (Lev. 25).
- **Seven feasts of the Lord** (Lev. 23): Passover, Unleavened Bread, Firstfruits, Pentecost, Trumpets, Atonement, Tabernacles.
- **Sevenfold sprinkling of blood**, seven trumpets of Jericho (Josh. 6), Naaman's seven dippings (2 Kings 5), seven pillars of Wisdom's house (Prov. 9:1), seven eyes of the Lord (Zech. 4:10).
- **Book of Revelation**: seven churches, seven seals, seven trumpets, seven bowls, seven thunders, seven stars, seven spirits before the throne, seven-headed dragon — the densest cluster of heptads in scripture (late 1st c. CE).
- **Seven gifts of the Holy Spirit** (wisdom, understanding, counsel, fortitude, knowledge, piety, fear of the Lord) — from Isaiah 11:2, codified by Aquinas.
- **Seven deadly sins** (pride, greed, lust, envy, gluttony, wrath, sloth) — John Cassian (5th c.) and Gregory the Great (6th c.). Paired with **seven heavenly virtues** (chastity, temperance, charity, diligence, patience, kindness, humility).
- **Seven sacraments** (Catholic/Orthodox; Council of Trent, 1547).
- **Seven corporal works of mercy** (Matthew 25 plus Tobit) and **seven spiritual works of mercy** — medieval pastoral theology.
- **Seven sorrows** and **seven joys of Mary** — Servite devotion, 13th c.
- **Seven last words of Christ** — harmonized homiletic tradition, possibly originating with Bede.
- **Seven Sleepers of Ephesus**: youths who slept 187 (or ~200) years in a cave; story popularized by Gregory of Tours (6th c.) and retold in Surah Al-Kahf of the Qur'an, where it shapes Islamic eschatology.
- **Seven canonical hours** of the Divine Office (Matins, Lauds, Prime, Terce, Sext, None, Vespers, Compline — actually eight when Matins/Vigils is counted separately; Psalm 119:164's "seven times a day" gave the count).
- **Seven Champions of Christendom** (medieval romance: St. George of England, etc.).
- **Seven Ecumenical Councils** of undivided Christendom (Nicaea I 325 – Nicaea II 787).
- **Seven oft-repeated verses** (*al-sabʿ al-mathānī*) — the Fatihah (Qur'an 15:87).
- **Seven gates of hell** (*Jahannam*) and **seven earths** in Islamic cosmography.
- **Seven circumambulations** (*tawaf*) of the Kaaba and the seven runs of *saʿy* between Safa and Marwa during Hajj.
- **Seven Sefirot of construction** in Kabbalah (Hesed through Malkhut), the lower seven of the ten; the basis of the shmita/sefirah cycle in your deep-dive.

## 3. Dharmic, East Asian & Steppe Sevens

- **Saptarishi**: the seven mind-born Vedic sages (variously listed; Mahabharata-era).
- **Saptamatrika**: the Seven Mothers (Brahmani, Vaishnavi, Maheshwari, Kaumari, Varahi, Indrani, Chamunda); guardian goddess group, c. 4th–8th c. CE temple iconography.
- **Saptapadi**: the seven steps/seven vows around the fire in a Hindu wedding (Vedic).
- **Sapta Dhatu**: the seven tissues (chyle, blood, flesh, fat, bone, marrow, semen) of Ayurveda; also a secondary list of seven metals (gold, silver, copper, iron, zinc, tin, lead).
- **Seven chakras** of tantric yoga (Muladhara to Sahasrara) — although the canonical count varies (some lineages name five, six, nine, or twelve), the seven-chakra ladder fixed by 16th-c. *Sat-Cakra-Nirupana* dominates modern usage.
- **Seven steps of the newborn Buddha**, who walked seven paces in each cardinal direction at birth.
- **Sapta Bojjhanga** / **Seven Factors of Awakening** in Theravada (mindfulness, investigation, energy, joy, tranquility, concentration, equanimity) — Pali Canon, Satipatthana Sutta.
- **Seven Jewels of the Chakravartin** (precious wheel, elephant, horse, jewel, queen, minister, general) — Mahayana iconography of the universal monarch.
- **Seven Buddhas of the Past** (Manushi Buddhas) — Vipassi through Shakyamuni; canonical in Theravada.
- **Seven offering bowls** on a Tibetan Buddhist altar (water, water, flowers, incense, light, perfume, food).
- **Seven Sages of the Bamboo Grove** (竹林七賢): Ji Kang, Ruan Ji, Liu Ling, Ruan Xian, Xiang Xiu, Wang Rong, Shan Tao — 3rd-c. CE Daoist literati, archetype of the recluse-poet.
- **Shichifukujin / Seven Lucky Gods** of Japan (Ebisu, Daikoku, Bishamonten, Benzaiten, Hotei, Fukurokuju, Jurōjin) — assembled in the Muromachi period (15th c.), riding the *takarabune* treasure ship.
- **Seven Stars of the Northern Dipper** (北斗七星): worshipped in Daoism as the bureau that records lifespans; key in *Shangqing* and *Lingbao* ritual.
- **Tanabata**: the Star Festival, originally the Chinese Qixi, marking the once-a-year meeting of the Weaver Girl (Vega) and Cowherd (Altair) on the seventh night of the seventh moon — first attested in Han-dynasty texts.
- **Chiljeolje / Chilseok**: Korean and Vietnamese cognates of the same seventh-night festival.
- **Seven generations principle** in Haudenosaunee (Iroquois) and Lakota political and ethical thought.

## 4. Mystical, Esoteric & Initiatic Sevens

- **Seven Valleys** of Sufi Farid ud-Din Attar's *Mantiq al-Tayr* (Conference of the Birds, c. 1177): Search, Love, Knowledge, Detachment, Unity, Wonderment, Annihilation. Reused by Baháʾuʾlláh in *The Seven Valleys* (1860).
- **Seven Maqāmāt / stations** of the Sufi path, codified by al-Sarrāj (d. 988) in *Kitāb al-Lumaʿ*: tawba, waraʿ, zuhd, faqr, ṣabr, tawakkul, riḍā.
- **Seven Nafs** (stages of soul) in later Sufi psychology: ammāra, lawwāma, mulhama, muṭmaʾinna, rāḍiya, marḍiyya, kāmila.
- **Seven Heavens of the Miʿrāj** (covered) and the seven prophets met therein.
- **Seven planetary archons** of the Gnostic *Apocryphon of John* (Yaldabaoth's hebdomad, 2nd c. CE) and the seven ages of Valentinian aeons.
- **Seven Hermetic principles** (Mentalism, Correspondence, Vibration, Polarity, Rhythm, Cause and Effect, Gender) — codified in *The Kybalion* (1908), a modern compilation presented as ancient.
- **Seven Rays** of Theosophy (Blavatsky, *The Secret Doctrine*, 1888; elaborated by Alice Bailey).
- **Seven principles of man** in Theosophy (physical, etheric, astral, mental, causal, buddhic, atmic).
- **Seven metals / seven planets / seven operations** of alchemy (calcination, dissolution, separation, conjunction, fermentation, distillation, coagulation) — *Theatrum Chemicum*, 16th–17th c.
- **Seven degrees** of the Mithraic mysteries: Corax, Nymphus, Miles, Leo, Perses, Heliodromus, Pater — attested in mithraea c. 100–400 CE.
- **Seven seals of Solomon** in late-antique magical literature.
- **Seven palaces (Hekhalot)** of Merkavah ascent literature (3rd–8th c. CE).

## 5. Mythological & Folkloric Sevens

- **Seven Against Thebes**: Polyneices and six allied champions besieging seven gates — Aeschylus's tragedy (467 BCE), Apollodorus.
- **Seven gates of Thebes**: matched one champion to each gate; mythographic geography earlier than the play.
- **Seven Sages of Greece** (Solon, Thales, Pittacus, Bias, Cleobulus, Chilon, Periander) — listed by Plato in *Protagoras* (4th c. BCE); the maxims at Delphi were ascribed to them.
- **Seven Wonders of the Ancient World**: enumerated by Antipater of Sidon (c. 140 BCE) — Pyramid of Giza, Hanging Gardens of Babylon, Statue of Zeus at Olympia, Temple of Artemis at Ephesus, Mausoleum at Halicarnassus, Colossus of Rhodes, Lighthouse of Alexandria.
- **Seven kings of Rome** (Romulus through Tarquinius Superbus) — Livy.
- **Seven daughters of Atlas** (Pleiades) and **seven daughters of Pleione**; **seven gates of the Theban polis**.
- **Seven Wise Masters / Seven Sages of Rome**: medieval Indo-Persian frame-tale cycle in which seven sages each tell a delaying story (Latinized c. 12th c.).
- **Seven Scorpions of Isis** (Tefen, Befen, Mestet, Mestetef, Petet, Tjetet, Matet) — Metternich Stela, 30th Dynasty (4th c. BCE).
- **Seven against the Mound** in the *Mabinogi*: Brân the Blessed's seven survivors of the Irish war.
- **Sebettu / Seven** as evil winds, demons, or sages-counselors at Mesopotamian banquets (3rd–1st millennium BCE); also the **seven** *apkallu* sages, half-fish creatures who taught civilization to antediluvian Sumerians.
- **Seven Lords of Xibalba** in the Popol Vuh (One Death, Seven Death, plus the diseases Flying Scab, Blood Gatherer, etc.); the bird-deity **Seven Macaw (Vucub Caquix)** and his twin sons (Quiche Maya, 16th-c. text on older traditions).
- **Chicomoztoc**: the Seven Caves from which the Nahua/Aztec ancestral tribes emerged (Codex Boturini, 16th c.).
- **Seven Cities of Cibola / Antillia**: Iberian-American gold mirage that lured Coronado (1540).
- **Seven Champions of Christendom** — Richard Johnson, 1596.
- **Seven Swabians** of the Brothers Grimm; **Seven Ravens**; **Seven Dwarves** of Snow White (KHM 53, 1812); **Seven-League Boots** of Perrault's *Hop-o'-My-Thumb* (1697); **Wolf and the Seven Kids**; **Bluebeard's seven wives** (Perrault, 1697); **Sinbad's seven voyages** (*One Thousand and One Nights*).
- **Seven-headed dragon/serpent**: from Lotan/Leviathan of Ugaritic and biblical mythology (KTU 1.5; Ps. 74) through Russian Zmey Gorynych to the Hydra and the Apocalypse.

## 6. Indigenous & Vernacular Sevens

- **Oceti Sakowin**, the Seven Council Fires of the Dakota/Lakota/Nakota nation; **Seven Sacred Rites** brought by White Buffalo Calf Woman (Inipi, Hanbleceyapi, Wiwanyang Wacipi, Hunkapi, Isnati Awicalowanpi, Tapa Wankayeyapi, Nagi Gluhapi). **Seven Generations** principle of ethical reach.
- **Seven Clans of the Cherokee** (Wolf, Deer, Bird, Paint, Blue, Long Hair, Wild Potato) — pre-contact matrilineal structure.
- **Seven Fires Prophecy** of the Anishinaabe (Ojibwe), foretelling seven epochs.
- **Seven Dakini directions** in Tibetan/Bön shamanic geography.
- **Seven African Powers** (Eleguá, Ogún, Obatalá, Yemayá, Oshún, Shangó, Oyá) — Cuban Lucumí/Santería syncretism, 19th–20th c.
- **Seven valleys of Goa** worshipped as the **Sat Bhaini** (seven sisters), local cognates of the Saptamatrika.

## 7. Body, Mind & Medical Sevens

- **Seven chakras** and **seven dhatus** (above).
- **Hippocratic-Galenic critical days**: the seventh day of an illness was diagnostic. Hippocrates, *Epidemics*; carried into Islamic and medieval medicine.
- **Seven ages of man** (Shakespeare, *As You Like It* II.vii, 1599; drawing on Ptolemy's seven planetary ages and Hippocratic seven-year stages).
- **Climacteric years** at multiples of seven (especially 49 and 63) in Renaissance medicine.
- **Seven openings of the head** (two eyes, two ears, two nostrils, mouth) — Pythagorean and Galenic.
- **Miller's "Magical Number Seven, Plus or Minus Two"** — George A. Miller, *Psychological Review*, 1956: working-memory capacity.
- **Seven primary emotions** in Paul Ekman's later expanded model (joy, sadness, anger, fear, surprise, disgust, contempt).
- **Seven dwarves' diseases**: a folk variant on the seven humours/disorders.

## 8. Arts, Letters & Sciences Sevens

- **Seven Liberal Arts**: the *trivium* (grammar, dialectic, rhetoric) and *quadrivium* (arithmetic, geometry, music, astronomy) — codified by Martianus Capella (5th c.), Cassiodorus, and Isidore of Seville; the structural backbone of medieval university curriculum.
- **Seven notes of the diatonic scale** (do-re-mi-fa-sol-la-ti) — Guido of Arezzo's solmization, 11th c.
- **Seven modes** of Western ecclesiastical music (Ionian, Dorian, Phrygian, Lydian, Mixolydian, Aeolian, Locrian) and the Persian **haft dastgah**.
- **Seven colors of the rainbow** — Newton's *Opticks* (1704), pegged at seven partly to harmonize with the diatonic scale.
- **Seven basic plots** (Christopher Booker, 2004): overcoming the monster, rags to riches, the quest, voyage and return, comedy, tragedy, rebirth.
- **Seven Pillars of Wisdom** — T. E. Lawrence's 1922 title, lifted from Proverbs 9:1.
- **Seven Samurai** — Kurosawa, 1954; remade as *The Magnificent Seven*, 1960.
- **Seven Habits of Highly Effective People** — Stephen Covey, 1989.
- **Seven Pillars of Self-Esteem** — Nathaniel Branden, 1994.

## 9. Numerological, Calendrical & Astronomical Sevens

- **Seven-day week**: of Mesopotamian origin (lunar-quarter approximation), institutionalized through Jewish Sabbath observance and Roman planetary days (Vettius Valens, 2nd c. CE; Constantine, 321 CE). Adopted with rare gaps by virtually every literate civilization.
- **Sumerian "seven"** (*imin*) as a generic term for completion; lul-7 ("seven lies") attested in Early Dynastic Proverbs (26th c. BCE).
- **Forty-nine-day mourning** (seven sevens) in Tibetan and Chinese Buddhism (the *bardo* of 49 days) and in Jewish *Shavuot* counting.
- **Shiva**: seven days (or thirty) of intense Jewish mourning beginning at burial.
- **Saptaha**: the seven-day Hindu yajna or recitation cycle (Bhagavata Saptaha).
- **Seven-year sabbatical** and the Jubilee of seven sevens.
- **Mayan calendar's seven steps** on the World Tree.
- **Pythagorean "seven"** as the *parthenos* (virgin) number — neither factor nor product within the decad — making it sacred to Athena.

## 10. Ritual, Ceremonial & Material Sevens

- **Saptapadi** wedding circuits (above).
- **Seven blessings** (*Sheva Berachot*) recited at a Jewish wedding and for seven days after.
- **Seven days of Sukkot** and **seven days of Passover** (Pesach).
- **Norouz Haft-sin**: the seven *s*-items on the Persian New Year table (sabzeh, samanu, senjed, sir, sib, somāq, serkeh) — modern but rooted in Sasanian *haft chin*.
- **Seven offerings** in Tibetan Buddhist puja; **seven-branched candlestick** (Menorah) in Jewish liturgy.
- **Seven precious substances** (*saptaratna*) in Buddhist iconography: gold, silver, lapis lazuli, crystal, agate, ruby, carnelian (lists vary).
- **Seven-tiered ziggurat** of Etemenanki at Babylon (and the seven-tier model behind Dante's Mount Purgatory).

## 11. Modern, Pop-Cultural & Secular Sevens

- **Seven Summits**: highest peak of each continent (Bass list, Messner list — popularized 1985).
- **Seven Wonders of the Modern/New World** (Bernard Weber's 2007 New7Wonders vote; the older American Society of Civil Engineers' seven wonders of the modern world).
- **Seven Pillars of NATO**, **G7**, **Big Seven** banks — political and economic groupings whose number is partly accident, partly the lingering pull of completion-by-seven.
- **Seven seas of modern usage**: Arctic, North Atlantic, South Atlantic, North Pacific, South Pacific, Indian, Southern.
- **Seven continents** of the modern Anglophone model.
- **Seventh-inning stretch**, **Seven-Eleven**, **lucky 7** on slot reels — the residue of the sacred number in casino, sport, and consumer life.
- **Magnificent Seven** (1960, 2016) and **Seven Brides for Seven Brothers** (1954) — Hollywood's recurrent reach for the heptad.
- **"Se7en"** (Fincher, 1995) revives the deadly sins for the screen.

## Closing Note for the Hymnist

A few observations a writer of "A WEEK" may find useful. Sevens cluster most densely around three nodes: (a) **completion and totality** — the days, the heavens, the worlds, the gates; (b) **paths and stages** — chakras, valleys, maqāmāt, factors of awakening, ages of man; and (c) **personae and companies** — Sages, Sleepers, Sisters, Mothers, Champions, Lucky Gods. A cosmogonic hymn can therefore borrow not only the canonical day-by-day frame but also the *figural* heptads: seven sisters appearing at the threshold, seven scorpions guarding the goddess, seven sages presiding over the dawn, seven jewels laid at each gate. Note also that several traditions count the seventh as a *transcending* term rather than a final step: the Sabbath stands apart from the work-days, the seventh valley is annihilation, the crown chakra is the dissolution of the chakra system itself, and the seventh heaven in many traditions is empty — pure light, or God alone. This pattern — six of work and one of consummation, or six of progression and one of unsaying — recurs widely enough to be a structural option worth knowing.

Where my sources flag ambiguity I have noted it: the canonical list of Shichifukujin only stabilized in the 17th century, the Hermetic Seven Principles are an early-20th-century synthesis presented as ancient, the Seven Chakras are one tantric configuration among several, and the "Out-of-Africa" hypothesis that all Pleiades-as-seven-sisters myths share a common origin (Norris & Norris, 2021) is plausible but unproven. Treat such datings as the best current scholarly estimate rather than as certain attestation.
